Crush & Strain Honey Harvest


I typically begin harvesting the first honey in early June and continue to harvest each week through July. My honey is harvested from foundationless natural comb newly drawn by the bees each season.



Each comb of fully capped honey is individually selected at the optimal time for harvest.


My crush and stain harvests are done in small batches of three to five kilograms. I use a stainless steel bucket and large knife to slice the comb to release the honey.


I continue to slice the comb until the wax is cut into small particles and all of the honey is relased.


Each batch takes about 10 minutes to prepare and I usually do three to five batches each harvest day.


The crushed comb is poured into a food grade plastic bucket lined with a fiter bag.


The fiter bag is lifted from the plastic bucket allowing the honey to drain.


The honey is allowed to drain overnight until only the wax remains.


The drained honey is poured into glass jars.



The wax is later processed in a solar wax melter.


Frequent small batch harvests allow me to capture the subtle changes in appearance and flavor of the honey throughout the season.


My methods are more time consuming but yield the highest quality of raw natural honey.
